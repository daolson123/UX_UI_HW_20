Case Study

Northwest Augmentative Communication Society

A non-profit organization based in Seattle, Washington that represents the northwestern United States. Focusing on empowering individuals with a communication deficit, by connecting the with technology and other tools that allow them to use their voice through alternative ways like devices that they utilize to share their words.

My Contributions

In this project my main contributions were putting together a testing plan on the original website followed later in the project with how we would test the redesign. I also helped build and design UI assets throughout the site, like carousels and card overlays.

What we started with

The original website has a decent overall content organization, but users struggle to locate specific content due to poor content chunking, excessive text, and lack of visual balance. Users have to spend the majority of their time scanning through bodies of text to find the information they are looking for and can easily miss their target if they aren’t focused enough or aren’t scanning for the correct words.

In Summary

Summary

Paragraph 1: The current NWACS site has decent overall content organization, but users struggle to locate specific content due to poor content chunking, excessive text, and lack of visual balance.  Users have to spend the majority of their time scanning through bodies of text to find the information they are looking for and can easily miss their target if they aren’t focused enough or aren’t scanning for the correct words.

Paragraph 2: Scanning huge bodies of texts takes more time than having symbols, images, and key headings that are automatically recognized.  This extra work can prove frustrating for users and can even lead them to abandon the task altogether--as one of our users did.

Paragraph 3: Additionally, the current visual design aims to be simple and straight forward, but the bold red and white color scheme is loud, further adds to the overall sameness of information and makes it difficult to identify key information.

Definition/Ideation

Why...
Our goal in tackling these tasks is to reduce
user scanning fatigue and increase efficiency
of completing tasks on the NWACS site.  To
create a more enjoyable user experience
through visual design and graphics, and to
make this site accessible to all users.

In Summary

Redesing Tasks

Continuing off of our testing and evaluation outcomes:

Our biggest tasks were:
To centralize the social links to only one area.
Add in more imagery to break up the text and make the user experience more enjoyable.
Revamp the calendar and make it clickable.
Re-work buttons and links for consistency and to be in line with best design practices.
Include an easily recognizable search bar.
Reduce visual/textual noise by consolidating the secondary side navigation into the main header.

Prototyping

A Closer Look at Navigation: Main Navigation

The side menu was consolidated into a main navigation menu, and social media icons were pinned to the side of the viewport. A search bar was integrated into the main navigation making it easy to scan the site and find exactly what you are looking for. The menu is also fixed, so it can be accessed from any scroll point within the page.

A Closer Look at Navigation: Footer Navigation

The current footer is functional and the changes were minimal, with both desktop as well as mobile. Referring back to our reorganization of the side menu, some of those items were re-distributed to the footer including: contact us, privacy policy, and disclosures. A mission statement was simply added to increase visibility of the work of NWACS.
